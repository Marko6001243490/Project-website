Shea Tree

The African Butter Tree, commonly known as the Shea tree (Vitellaria paradoxa), is a remarkable and culturally significant tree native to the savanna regions of West and Central Africa.They are very resilient and can live up to 200–300 years.

Malabar Nut

Malabar Nut, also known as Adhatoda vasica or Justicia adhatoda, is a medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent. It's well known in traditional Ayurvedic, Siddha, and Unani systems of medicine for its powerful therapeutic properties, especially in treating respiratory conditions.

Wax Mallow

Wax Mallow is a common name for several species of flowering plants, but it's most often associated with Malvaviscus arboreus, a tropical shrub also known as Turk’s Cap, Sleeping Hibiscus, or Mexican Apple.Not as widely used medicinally as plants like Adhatoda vasica, but still has value in folk remedies.

Pipala Tree

The Pipala Tree, also known as the Sacred Fig, is one of the most revered trees in South Asia, especially in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism.It has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive long tapering tip; rustle even in the slightest breeze.

Tea plant

The tea plant, botanically known as Camellia sinensis, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East and South Asia, widely cultivated for its young leaves and leaf buds, which are used to produce tea — one of the most consumed beverages in the world.Tea plants thrive in warm, humid climates with well-draining, acidic soils, and are typically grown in tropical and subtropical regions, especially at higher altitudes, which enhance leaf quality.

Papaya

The papaya is a fast-growing, tropical fruit-bearing plant known for its sweet, orange-fleshed fruit and numerous health benefits. It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is native to Central America and southern Mexico, though it is now grown widely in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.Papaya plants can be male, female, or hermaphroditic (with both parts). The flowers are small, white to cream-colored, and fragrant.

Ti Plant

The Ti Plant is a striking, colorful plant with deep cultural and ornamental significance, especially in tropical regions. It’s prized for its vibrant foliage and is often associated with good luck and protection.An evergreen tropical shrub, often 3–10 feet tall in gardens, but can grow taller in native environments.

Banana

The banana plant is a large, fast-growing, herbaceous perennial, often mistaken for a tree due to its tall "trunk." What looks like a trunk is actually a pseudostem — a tightly packed cylinder of leaf sheaths.It's height ranges 2 to 9 meters (6–30 feet), depending on variety.

Kassod Tree

The Kassod Tree is a fast-growing tropical tree known for its hard wood, yellow flowers, and medicinal properties. It’s widely used in traditional medicine and agroforestry.It's wood is strong, dark-colored, and highly valued for furniture, construction, and firewood.

The Red Cotton Tree

The Red Cotton Tree is a majestic, flowering tree admired for its vibrant red blooms and historical medicinal uses. It's especially striking when in full bloom, with leafless branches covered in fiery red flowers
