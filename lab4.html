Shea Tree

The African Butter Tree, commonly known as the Shea tree (Vitellaria paradoxa), is a remarkable and culturally significant tree native to the savanna regions of West and Central Africa.They are very resilient and can live up to 200–300 years.

Four-leaf Clover

The four-leaf clover is a rare variation of the common three-leaf clover (Trifolium repens), also known as white clover.Used traditionally in teas for coughs and colds, or as a mild detoxifier.Acts as ground cover and natural fertilizer in permaculture because of its nitrogen-fixing roots

Grias

The Grias plant (especially species like Grias cauliflora, aka Anchovy Pear) may not be a household name, but it's got a pretty impressive list of uses — culinary, medicinal, and ecological.The fruit of Grias cauliflora is edible — long, brownish, and shaped like a mango or elongated pear.

Anacua

Anacua is a hardy and fascinating tree native to southern Texas and parts of Mexico. It's lesser known but plays a significant role in its native ecosystem and traditional uses.The berries are sweet and edible.The wood is hard and durable, though not widely harvested.

Mesua ferrea

Extracts from the flowers and leaves have antimicrobial effects, effective against bacterial and fungal infections.The bark decoction is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, and indigestion.Mesua ferrea contains flavonoids and other bioactive compounds that promote heart health.

Hydnocarpus

Hydnocarpus is a genus of medium to large trees belonging to the Achariaceae family. These trees are primarily found in Indochina, Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines.They produce chaulmoogra oil, historically used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine for treating leprosy and skin diseases

Hydnocarpus kurzii

Hydnocarpus kurzii is one of the key species in the Hydnocarpus genus, best known for producing chaulmoogra oil, which played a major role in early treatments for leprosy.H. kurzii was one of the primary sources of chaulmoogra oil used to treat leprosy in both Indian traditional medicine and early Western medicine.

Kamala Tree

The Kamala tree, also known as Mallotus philippensis, is a fascinating plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It's known for its vibrant red powder (kamala dye) found on the fruit, which has traditional, medicinal, and dyeing applications.Used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat tapeworms and other intestinal parasites.Applied externally for fungal infections.Sometimes used in herbal formulations for liver protection.It has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties (being explored in modern herbal research)

Chacruna

Chacruna refers to Psychotria viridis, a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest, known for its significant role in traditional South American shamanic practices. It is particularly famous for being one of the primary plant sources of DMT (dimethyltryptamine), a powerful psychedelic compound.Chacruna is most famously used in the preparation of ayahuasca, a traditional Amazonian brew made from the Banisteriopsis caapi vine and chacruna leaves.Chacruna contains DMT, a powerful psychoactive compound, which, when combined with MAOIs (monoamine oxidase inhibitors) in the Banisteriopsis caapi, allows the DMT to be orally active, inducing intense visual and spiritual experiences.

Malabar tree

The Malabar tree, commonly known as Malabar neem or Indian lilac, refers to Azadirachta indica, a tree native to the Indian subcontinent and other tropical regions. It's widely known for its medicinal properties and ecological benefits, as well as its use in traditional medicine and modern organic farming. Neem oil Extracted from the seeds of the tree, neem oil is famous for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties.
