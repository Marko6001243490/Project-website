Indian Rosewood

Indian Rosewood, also known as Dalbergia latifolia, is a prized hardwood native to the Indian subcontinent. It's known for its beautiful grain, rich color, durability, and versatility. Used for making high-end furniture pieces like tables, chairs, cabinets, and beds.

Nutgrass

Nutgrass, also known as Cyperus rotundus (and sometimes called purple nutsedge), is a hardy, grass-like weed—but despite its reputation in agriculture, it has a long history of medicinal and traditional uses, especially in Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), and folk medicine.Used to treat indigestion, diarrhea, and flatulence.

Mountain Yam

Mountain Yam can refer to a few different species, but most commonly it's used to describe Dioscorea opposita (also known as Dioscorea polystachya, Chinese yam, or Nagaimo in Japanese). It's a tuberous plant with a long history of use in traditional medicine and cuisine, especially in East Asia.Believed to strengthen the spleen and aid digestion.Used as a natural qi tonic in Traditional Chinese Medicine—boosts stamina and energy.

Wild Olive Tree

The wild olive tree (commonly referred to as Olea europaea var. sylvestris) is considered the wild ancestor of the cultivated olive tree. It's native to the Mediterranean region and holds both ecological and cultural importance.Thrives in arid, rocky soils, helping prevent erosion.Supports biodiversity by offering food and shelter to birds, insects, and small mammals.

Assam Gleditsia

Assam Gleditsia likely refers to a local or regional name for a species within the Gleditsia genus, commonly known as honey locusts. These are deciduous trees from the Fabaceae (legume) family, and while the most widely known species is Gleditsia triacanthos (native to North America), some Gleditsia species also grow in Asia—including India, China, and Southeast Asia.Used to treat: Skin diseases ,Respiratory issues like cough or asthma.

Alsophila khasyana

Alsophila khasyana (synonym: Cyathea khasyana) is a species of tree fern native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly Assam, Meghalaya, and Arunachal Pradesh. It belongs to the Cyatheaceae family, which includes many large, tropical ferns commonly referred to as tree ferns.Plays an important role in moist, shaded forest ecosystems, especially in subtropical and tropical forests.

Amla

Hydnocarpus kurzii is one of the key species in the Hydnocarpus genus, best known for producing chaulmoogra oil, which played a major role in early treatments for leprosy.H. kurzii was one of the primary sources of chaulmoogra oil used to treat leprosy in both Indian traditional medicine and early Western medicine.

Kamala Tree

The Kamala tree, also known as Mallotus philippensis, is a fascinating plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It's known for its vibrant red powder (kamala dye) found on the fruit, which has traditional, medicinal, and dyeing applications.Used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat tapeworms and other intestinal parasites.Applied externally for fungal infections.Sometimes used in herbal formulations for liver protection.It has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties (being explored in modern herbal research)

Chacruna

Chacruna refers to Psychotria viridis, a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest, known for its significant role in traditional South American shamanic practices. It is particularly famous for being one of the primary plant sources of DMT (dimethyltryptamine), a powerful psychedelic compound.Chacruna is most famously used in the preparation of ayahuasca, a traditional Amazonian brew made from the Banisteriopsis caapi vine and chacruna leaves.Chacruna contains DMT, a powerful psychoactive compound, which, when combined with MAOIs (monoamine oxidase inhibitors) in the Banisteriopsis caapi, allows the DMT to be orally active, inducing intense visual and spiritual experiences.

Malabar tree

The Malabar tree, commonly known as Malabar neem or Indian lilac, refers to Azadirachta indica, a tree native to the Indian subcontinent and other tropical regions. It's widely known for its medicinal properties and ecological benefits, as well as its use in traditional medicine and modern organic farming. Neem oil Extracted from the seeds of the tree, neem oil is famous for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties.
